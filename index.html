Sachin Tendulkar

The heart of Indian cricket.

Some greatest achievements in Sachin's cricketing journey:

  • Sachin made his debut for the Indian national cricket team at the age of 16 in a Test match against the fierce Pakistan bowling attack in 1989.
  • In the 2003 World Cup, Sachin Tendulkar was at his brilliant best, amassing 673 runs. His iconic 98 against Pakistan remains one of the most celebrated innings.
  • Tendulkar was the first player to score a double century in ODI cricket. He scored an unbeaten 200 against South Africa in February 2010.
  • Tendulkar’s international cricketing career spanned 24 years, from 1989 to 2013. He played in 664 matches across all formats, making him one of the longest-serving cricketers in history.
  • He also scored a historic unbeaten century at Lord’s Cricket Ground in London in 1990, becoming the youngest cricketer to do so at the age of 17.
  • Over the years, Sachin Tendulkar recieved many prestigious awards including Arjuna Award in 1994, Bharat Khel Ratna in 1997, Padma Shri Award in 1999, Padma Vibhushan in 2008 and Bharat Ratna in 2014.

...In the golden era of Indian cricket, Sachin Tendulkar was more than just a cricketer, he was a beacon of hope and inspiration for millions of Indians.
